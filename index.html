Exploring Leading Causes of Mortality in Australia (2016-2022)

      This interactive stacked area chart presents a visual overview of the top 7 leading causes of mortality in Australia from 2016 to 2022. Designed to highlight both long-term health trends and emerging health challenges, the chart enables users to explore how different causes of death have changed over time, with the option to filter the data by gender. The seven causes of death presented - ordered from most to least impactful are:

  1. Malignant neoplasms
  2. Ischaemic heart diseases
  3. Cerebrovascular diseases
  4. Dementia
  5. Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  6. Diabetes mellitus
  7. COVID-19

Australia Proportion of Mortality Cause (2016-2022) stacked area

Measure: per 100,000 inhabitants; Sex: Total

      Each area in the chart corresponds to one of these causes, showing their relative contributions to overall mortality by year. The stacked format allows for easy comparison of both individual trends and total mortality volume.

      A notable feature of the chart is the visible emergence of COVID-19 in 2021, marking a shift in Australia's health landscape. While COVID-19 appears as the smallest contributor among the top 7, its sudden rise reflects the global health crisis and its impact on mortality statistics during this period.

      By interacting with the chart, users can isolate patterns by gender and observe how different causes affected men and women differently over the years. The tool serves as an accessible entry point for understanding public health dynamics in Australia across a seven-year span.
